Magnetic anomaly identifications - Nakanishi et al. (1992)

Magnetic anomaly identifications between M0 and M29 have been assigned ages in Gee and Kent (2007) (GeeK2007).
As GeeK2007 does not provide ages beyond M29, magnetic anomaly identifications pre-M29 (i.e. M30r to M35r) have
been assigned ages based on a 'hybrid' version of the Tominaga et al 2008 timescale, where 0.07 Ma has been subtracted
from each published age to account for the difference between the last available chron in GeeK2007 (M29r, 157.530 Ma)
and Tominaga et al (2008)/Tivey et al. (2006) (M29r, 157.6 Ma). See below for a comparison of the timescales from
GeeK2007, Tominaga et al (2008), Tivey et al. (2006), and calculation into the 'hybrid' timescale (second timescale).

Author: Nicky Wright, EarthByte, U of Sydney, March 2014.
Updated June 2016: Hybrid timescale values are given relative to Tivey et al. (2006) instead of Tominaga et al. (2008).
These timescales only differ in their use of decimal places.
